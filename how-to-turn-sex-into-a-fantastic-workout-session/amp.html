Frequently Asked Questions

Can sex really count as a workout?
Yes, sex can be a fun way to burn calories and engage various muscle groups, contributing to your overall fitness.
How many calories can I burn during sex?
On average, you can burn between 85 to 150 calories in a 30-minute session, depending on intensity and positions.
Which muscles are worked during sex?
Sex engages core muscles, pelvic floor muscles, glutes, and even arms and legs depending on the positions.
How can I make sex a better workout?
Try incorporating more movement, varied positions, and focus on maintaining your core and balance to increase intensity.
Is it safe to exercise during sex if I have health issues?
It's best to consult with your doctor, but generally low-intensity sex is safe; listen to your body and avoid overexertion.
Can sex improve my pelvic floor strength?
Yes, regular sexual activity can strengthen pelvic floor muscles, enhancing control and pleasure.
How do I stay motivated to enjoy sex as exercise?
Focus on the pleasure and connection, and think of it as a fun way to stay active rather than a chore.
Can sex replace my regular workout routine?
Sex adds to your physical activity but shouldn’t completely replace structured workouts for overall fitness.
What positions burn the most calories during sex?
Positions that involve more movement, like standing or active missionary, tend to burn more calories.
How can I prevent soreness after an active sex session?
Warm up beforehand, stay hydrated, and stretch gently afterward to reduce soreness and improve recovery.
